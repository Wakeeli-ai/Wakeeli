## Content Patterns and Strategy

### Posting Patterns

The top B2B and PropTech LinkedIn creators share these habits:

- **Frequency:** 2 to 5 posts per week minimum. Jason Lemkin posts 14 times per week. Most top creators land between 3 and 5 substantive posts per week. Fewer than 2 per week produces minimal compounding effect.
- **Consistency over intensity:** A founder who posts 3 times per week every week for 12 months will always outperform one who posts 20 times in one month and then disappears. The algorithm rewards regularity.
- **Personal profiles, not company pages:** Employee and founder content gets 8x more engagement than company page posts. 90% of SaaS companies post exclusively from their company page. Fox posting as Fox will always outperform Wakeeli posting as Wakeeli.
- **Morning posting:** Tuesday through Thursday, between 7 AM and 11 AM local time, consistently performs best for B2B audiences. For Fox in Beirut, this aligns with the Lebanese work morning and the European work day starting simultaneously.
- **No links in the post body:** External links reduce reach by 25 to 35%. Post the link in the first comment instead, or edit the post to add the link after initial engagement has built.

---

### Common Hooks

The hook is the first 2 lines of a LinkedIn post. LinkedIn truncates after roughly 140 characters. If the hook does not create immediate curiosity, the rest of the post does not get read.

The 7 hook types that consistently work across B2B SaaS and PropTech:

**1. The data drop**
Lead with a number that is either shocking or counterintuitive.
Example: "87% of real estate leads go to the first agent who responds. Most agents respond in 4 hours."
Why it works: Numbers create instant credibility and imply a problem that needs solving.

**2. The contrarian take**
Challenge a belief the audience holds.
Example: "PropTech does not have an adoption problem. It has a trust problem."
Why it works: Disagreement stops the scroll. People want to read why you think this.

**3. The vulnerability open**
Start with a failure or mistake.
Example: "We almost shut down 6 months in. Here's what saved us."
Why it works: Vulnerability is rare on LinkedIn. It creates immediate emotional connection.

**4. The audience-specific call-out**
Name your exact reader in the first line.
Example: "If you're a real estate agency owner in Lebanon who is losing leads at night: this is for you."
Why it works: The reader feels directly addressed. Engagement from the right people skyrockets.

**5. The bold prediction**
Make a specific, dated claim about the market.
Example: "Within 3 years, Lebanese real estate agencies that are not using AI to manage leads will not survive the agencies that are."
Why it works: Predictions invite agreement, disagreement, and debate. All of these are engagement.

**6. The story open**
Start in the middle of a real event.
Example: "I was on a call with a Beirut agency yesterday. They told me they get 40 leads a day. I asked how many they follow up with. The answer was 12."
Why it works: Stories create forward momentum. People want to know what happened next.

**7. The "how I" framework**
Lead with a first-person result.
Example: "How I went from 0 to 5 pilot clients in Lebanon in 90 days without spending a dollar on ads."
Why it works: Promises a transferable lesson. High save and share rate.

**What is different about the MENA LinkedIn audience:**
