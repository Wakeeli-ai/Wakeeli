## SECTION 2: THE BRAND VOICE OF WAKEELI

### The Core Distinction

Fox's personal page is a person. Wakeeli's company page is a business. They sound different on purpose.

| Dimension | Fox's Personal Page | Wakeeli Company Page |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Founder. Opinionated. Human. | Brand. Clear. Authoritative. |
| Perspective | "I built this because..." | "Here is what the data shows..." |
| Content type | Stories, insights, opinions | Stats, proof, product, results |
| Voice | First person singular | Third person or "we" |
| Style | Raw, real, sometimes vulnerable | Precise, confident, data-forward |
| Purpose | Build trust in Fox | Build trust in Wakeeli |
| Emotion | Personal conviction | Market conviction |
| Risk tolerance | High (polarizing opinions welcome) | Medium (bold but backed) |

---

### Same Topic, Two Treatments

**Topic: Speed of response**

**Fox's personal post:**
> I used to think real estate was about knowing the most. Then I watched an agency lose a $400,000 deal because their agent saw the WhatsApp message 47 minutes late. The lead had already toured with a competitor. That is when I started building Wakeeli.

**Wakeeli company page post:**
> 35 to 50% of real estate sales go to the first responder. That is not a theory. That is Forbes research. In Lebanon, most agencies respond to WhatsApp leads in hours, not minutes. Wakeeli responds in seconds. The agencies using it are not working harder. They are just responding first.

Same data point. Same product. Completely different voice.

---

**Topic: Product feature (tour booking)**

**Fox's personal post:**
> Three months ago I sat down with an agent in Achrafieh. She was booking 4 tours a week manually, managing it all on paper. Today her AI handles 12 bookings without her lifting a finger. She spends the time she recovered on closings. This is what I mean when I say AI should remove friction, not add it.

**Wakeeli company page post:**
> When a lead says "I want to see the apartment," the deal has momentum. Wakeeli captures it immediately. Automatic tour booking: the AI collects lead availability, matches it to agent calendars, confirms the appointment, and sends reminders. Zero manual coordination. No momentum lost.

---

### Voice Rules for the Company Page

1. Lead with data or outcomes, never with features
2. Never say "chatbot." Say "conversion system" or "AI engine"
3. Never say "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," or "game-changing"
4. Avoid exclamation points. Confidence does not need them
5. Keep sentences short. One idea per sentence
6. "We" is acceptable but use it sparingly. "Wakeeli" is often stronger
7. Never use passive voice in a claim. "Wakeeli converts leads" not "leads are converted by Wakeeli"
8. Every post should have one specific number, stat, or concrete detail
9. CTA at the end of every post. Never assume the reader knows what to do next
10. No em dashes. No double dashes. Use commas, colons, or periods instead

## SECTION 3: CONTENT PILLARS

The company page posts from five content pillars. Each pillar serves a different purpose in the buyer journey. Rotate through them intentionally.

---

### Pillar 1: Market Data and Industry Stats

**Purpose:** Establish Wakeeli as the most informed voice on Lebanese real estate lead behavior.

**What to post:**
- Lebanese real estate market statistics (transaction volume, price trends, geographic demand shifts)
- Response time data and its impact on conversion
- WhatsApp usage stats among Lebanese consumers
- Global proptech benchmarks applied to the Lebanese market
- Forbes, NAR, and industry report data with Lebanese context applied

**Tone:** Factual. No editorializing. Let the numbers carry the weight.

**Example hook formats:**
- "47 minutes. That is the average response time for a WhatsApp real estate inquiry in Lebanon."
- "In 2025, 78% of Lebanese property searches started on a mobile device."
- "Forbes measured it. 35 to 50% of real estate sales go to the first responder."

**Frequency:** 2 posts per week from this pillar

---

### Pillar 2: Product Capabilities

**Purpose:** Show exactly what Wakeeli does without being a product brochure.

**What to post:**
- Feature spotlights with specific use cases
- Before/after workflow comparisons (agency without Wakeeli vs. with Wakeeli)
- How specific parts of the platform work (lead scoring, tour booking, WhatsApp integration)
- New feature announcements framed as problems solved, not features added

**Tone:** Precise. Functional. Show the mechanism, not just the benefit.

**Key rule:** Never post a feature without anchoring it to an outcome. "Wakeeli qualifies leads automatically" is a feature. "Wakeeli qualifies leads automatically so your agents only pick up the phone when the deal is already half-closed" is a benefit.

**Frequency:** 1 to 2 posts per week from this pillar

---

### Pillar 3: Lebanese Real Estate Market Intelligence

**Purpose:** Give agency owners and brokers a reason to follow the page beyond just Wakeeli news.

**What to post:**
- Area-specific demand reports (Achrafieh, Jnah, Metn, Jounieh, etc.)
- Buyer behavior patterns in Lebanon post-2023
- Seasonal transaction trends (when deals close, when inquiries peak)
- Rental vs. sales market data
- Off-plan vs. ready unit demand shifts
- Commentary on specific neighborhoods gaining or losing traction

**Tone:** Analytical. No opinions. Data first, context second.

**Note:** This pillar builds the audience before they are buyers. An agency owner who relies on Wakeeli's page for market intelligence will remember Wakeeli when they need a conversion tool.

**Frequency:** 1 post per week from this pillar

---

### Pillar 4: Client Results and Social Proof

**Purpose:** Prove that Wakeeli works with real outcomes from real agencies.

**What to post:**
- Client case studies (agency name, challenge, results with numbers)
- Testimonial quotes formatted as visuals
- Aggregate stats from the platform ("Wakeeli agencies responded to 2,400 leads last month")
- Milestone posts framed as client wins ("100 tours booked through Wakeeli in March")
- Before/after comparison: how an agency's response time changed

**Tone:** Proof-first. Let the client's results do the talking. Keep Wakeeli's voice neutral and factual in these posts.

**Note:** In the early months before clients, use platform activity stats, beta tester feedback, or anonymized results. Never fabricate a testimonial. Placeholder content is better than false social proof.

**Frequency:** 1 post per week from this pillar (or as results become available)

---

### Pillar 5: Behind the Build

**Purpose:** Build technical credibility and show the depth of the product.

**What to post:**
- Engineering decisions and why they were made
- Why Wakeeli is built specifically for the Lebanese market (not a generic Western tool repurposed)
- WhatsApp Business API architecture (high level, no proprietary detail)
- AI model behavior and how it handles edge cases in Lebanese real estate conversations
- Data security and privacy approach
- Platform reliability stats (uptime, response latency)

**Tone:** Technical but accessible. Agency owners do not need to understand the API. They need to understand that this was built carefully and will not break.

**Frequency:** 1 post every 2 weeks from this pillar

## SECTION 5: POSTING CADENCE AND TIMING

### Company Page vs. Personal Page

| | Wakeeli Company Page | Fox's Personal Page |
|---|---|---|
| Posts per week | 3 to 4 | 5 to 7 |
| Post length | Medium (150 to 300 words) | Variable (short to long) |
| Voice | Brand voice | Fox's personal voice |
| Primary goal | Credibility and awareness | Engagement and reach |
| Secondary goal | Lead generation | Wakeeli brand awareness |

**Why the company page posts less:** Company pages get lower organic reach than personal profiles on LinkedIn. Posting 5 times per week on a company page with no follower base is wasted effort. Quality over frequency until the page has 500+ followers.

---

### Best Times to Post (Lebanese B2B Audience, Asia/Beirut Timezone)

**Primary window:**
- Tuesday to Thursday, 8:30 AM to 10:00 AM (agency owners checking feeds before client calls)
- Tuesday to Thursday, 12:30 PM to 1:30 PM (lunch scroll)

**Secondary window:**
- Sunday 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM (Lebanese business owners planning the week ahead)
- Monday 8:00 AM to 9:30 AM (start-of-week attention)

**Avoid:**
- Friday afternoon (Lebanese weekend begins, attention drops)
- Saturday (lowest B2B engagement)
- Late evenings on weekdays (personal content dominates, business content buried)

**Note:** These are starting benchmarks. After 60 days of posting, review LinkedIn Page Analytics to see when Wakeeli's specific audience is most active. Adjust based on real data, not generic best practices.

---

### Weekly Posting Rhythm

| Day | Post Type | Pillar |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | Stat Drop or Market Intelligence | Pillar 1 or 3 |
| Wednesday | Feature Spotlight or Client Result | Pillar 2 or 4 |
| Friday | Behind the Build or Market Data | Pillar 5 or 1 |
| (Optional) Sunday | Market Intelligence Brief | Pillar 3 |

This rhythm gives the page a predictable cadence: educate on Tuesday, prove on Wednesday, build credibility on Friday.

## SECTION 7: COMPANY PAGE GROWTH STRATEGY

### The Reality of Company Page Growth

Company pages grow slower than personal profiles on LinkedIn. The algorithm prioritizes personal content. Accept this and plan around it.

The company page is not the primary reach engine. Fox's personal profile is. The company page is the credibility destination. When someone discovers Fox's content, follows him, and gets curious about Wakeeli, they check the company page. It needs to be impressive when they land there.

---

### Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1 to 2)

Goal: 200 followers, 10 posts live, complete page setup

Actions:
- Complete 100% of page fields (description, specialties, logo, banner, CTA)
- Post 3 times per week from the five content pillars
- Fox shares every company page post to his personal feed with a brief personal comment (this is the primary follower acquisition method)
- Tag Wakeeli in every personal post that mentions the business
- Add the Wakeeli company page to Fox's personal profile "Experience" section
- Connect with all first-degree connections who are in Lebanese real estate

---

### Phase 2: Audience Building (Month 3 to 4)

Goal: 500 followers, 2 percent engagement rate, first inbound leads from LinkedIn

Actions:
- Begin sponsored content (boosted posts) targeting: job title "Real Estate Agent" and "Real Estate Broker" in Lebanon, Beirut area, 5km to 30km radius
- Budget: $200 to $400/month in the first two months. Data-dependent after that.
- Identify the 3 top-performing organic posts and boost them
- Start commenting on posts from Lebanese real estate industry accounts to build brand visibility
- Reach out to the 7 target agencies directly from the company page (warm them up before a sales call)

---

### Phase 3: Credibility Compounding (Month 5+)

Goal: 1,000 followers, consistent inbound, page as a sales asset

Actions:
- Post client case studies as they come in
- Add team members to the page as the company hires (each new follower added to a company page = their network sees the page)
- Begin LinkedIn newsletter from the company page: "Lebanese Real Estate Intelligence" weekly brief
- Cross-promote with agencies and proptech partners (reshares, co-authored posts)
- Pursue LinkedIn "Company to Follow" features in relevant newsletters

---

## SECTION 8: CROSS-REFERENCING STRATEGY

### The Personal + Company Flywheel

The goal is a self-reinforcing system where:
- Fox's personal reach drives traffic to the Wakeeli company page
- The Wakeeli company page provides credibility when people land there
- Company page content gives Fox material to riff on personally
- Personal posts about Wakeeli drive company page followers

This only works if both pages are active and strategically linked.

---

### How Fox Tags Wakeeli from Personal Posts

**Rule 1:** Every post about the business mentions or tags Wakeeli explicitly.

Do this naturally:
- "I am building Wakeeli [tag the company page] to fix this..."
- "The team at Wakeeli [tag] published a breakdown of this data on our page today..."
- "Wakeeli [tag] just crossed a milestone I want to share..."

**Rule 2:** When Fox shares industry data on his personal page, he attributes it to Wakeeli's research.

Example: "Our team at Wakeeli [tag] pulled this data from WhatsApp response logs last week. The numbers are not pretty for most agencies."

This makes the company page the source, drives curiosity, converts personal followers into company page followers.

**Rule 3:** Personal milestone posts always end with a company page CTA.

Example: "If you want to follow what we are building, the Wakeeli company page [tag] gets weekly updates on product and Lebanese market data."

---

### How the Wakeeli Page References Fox

**Resharing Fox's posts:**
- When Fox publishes a post that directly relates to Wakeeli's core message, the company page reshares it with a 1-sentence company-voice comment
- Do not reshare every Fox post. Only the ones that add company page credibility.
- Ideal reshare frequency: 1 to 2 per month

**Example company page reshare comment:**
- Fox post: founder story about building Wakeeli after watching an agency miss a deal
- Company page reshare comment: "This is the moment Wakeeli was built to prevent. The first responder wins. Always."

---

### Content Coordination: No Overlap, Full Complement

The two pages should never say the same thing the same week. Coordinate posts so they:

- Cover different angles of the same topic
- Lead into each other (Fox teases something, company page delivers the data)
- Amplify without duplicating

**Coordination example:**

Monday: Fox personal post, "I am watching Lebanese agencies lose deals to WhatsApp response time. Here is the pattern I keep seeing." (storytelling, no stats)

Wednesday: Wakeeli company page, "The data behind response time and real estate conversion. Why 47 minutes costs agencies deals." (data post, links to the problem Fox described)

This creates a narrative thread across both channels for the same audience.

---

### Referral Loop

Every Wakeeli company page post should do one of two things:

1. Drive followers to follow Fox personally for more founder insight
2. Drive Fox's followers to the company page for more data and proof

Include these micro-CTAs naturally:
- End of company posts: "Fox [tag personal profile], Wakeeli's founder, goes deeper on this topic on his personal page."

### The Only Numbers That Matter for Business

LinkedIn analytics are interesting. These three numbers are what actually matter:

1. Inbound demo requests originating from LinkedIn (track via UTM parameters on all links)
2. Number of target agencies (the 7 initial targets) who are following the page
3. Number of direct messages or comments from agency owners asking about Wakeeli

Everything else is a vanity metric. Track it. Do not optimize for it.

### Monthly Analytics Review (30-Minute Process)

1. Check follower count and growth rate
2. Pull the 3 best-performing posts by impressions and engagement
3. Note what pillar and format they were
4. Adjust the next month's content to produce more of what worked
5. Check demographic data: are followers in the right geography and industry
6. Review CTR: which posts drove the most clicks to the website or demo page
7. Update content calendar based on findings

## SECTION 10: LONG-TERM BRAND POSITIONING

### What the Company Page Becomes Over Time

In 12 months, if the strategy is executed, the Wakeeli company page should be recognized as:

**The most data-informed LinkedIn voice on Lebanese real estate.**

Not the most popular. Not the most entertaining. The most accurate. The most useful. The one that agency owners share with colleagues because it contains information they cannot get elsewhere.

This positioning is sustainable and defensible. Fox's personal page handles inspiration and personality. The company page handles intelligence and proof.

---

### The Three-Stage Content Evolution

**Stage 1: Awareness (Months 1 to 3)**
Goal: Get Lebanese real estate professionals to know Wakeeli exists and understand what it does.
Content focus: Market data, product explainers, response time statistics.

**Stage 2: Consideration (Months 4 to 6)**
Goal: Give prospects enough proof to seriously consider a demo.
Content focus: Client case studies, platform metrics, ROI calculations, comparison of life with and without Wakeeli.

**Stage 3: Authority (Month 7+)**
Goal: Be the default reference point when anyone in Lebanese real estate discusses AI, lead conversion, or WhatsApp automation.
Content focus: Original research, quarterly market reports, industry partnership announcements, product roadmap previews.

### The Wakeeli LinkedIn Newsletter

Launch at month 3 when the page has at least 300 followers.

**Name:** "The Lebanese Real Estate Brief by Wakeeli"

**Format:** Monthly. 500 to 800 words. Data-forward.

**Contents:**
- 3 key Lebanese real estate data points from the month
- 1 lead conversion insight (always with data)
- 1 Wakeeli platform update or milestone
- 1 featured client result or agency story

This newsletter positions Wakeeli as a publisher, not just a vendor. Agency owners who subscribe get value before they ever pay a dollar. When they are ready to convert leads faster, Wakeeli is already top of mind.

### Tone at Scale

As Wakeeli grows, the company page tone stays consistent:

**Bold but backed.** Every claim has a number behind it.
